### 1.2 Detailed User Profiles

#### 👷 Architect (Primary B2B User)
**Technical depth:** Expert  
**Domain skills they bring:**
- Floor plan principles: circulation, zoning, room adjacency, spatial flow
- Building codes: NBC India 2016, local municipal bylaws, fire codes
- FSI/FAR: Floor Space Index, coverage ratio, setbacks
- Structural grids: column bay spacing, load path planning
- Natural light & ventilation: window sizing, cross-ventilation, solar angles
- Drawing standards: AIA/NBC, scale notation, title blocks

**What they expect from the tool:**
- Professional DXF export (AIA layer standards, line weights, door arcs)
- Setback enforcement from plot boundary
- Column grid overlay
- Corridor width validation (min 3.5 ft per NBC)
- Built-up vs carpet area calculation
- Compliance checklist UI
- Multi-wing / courtyard layout support

**Why they'd reject it today:** DXF output violates industry standards. No setback/code enforcement.

#### 🧘 Vastu Shastra Practitioner (Differentiating User)
**Technical depth:** Deep domain knowledge, moderate tech
**Domain skills they bring:**
- Vastu Purusha Mandala (81-grid or 64-grid sacred geometry)
- 16-zone Shodasha Pada analysis (not just 8 compass directions)
- Pancha Bhuta: Fire (SE), Water (NE/N), Earth (SW/Center), Air (NW), Space (Center)
- Brahmasthana: central void — must be open and unencumbered
- Vastu Dwar (entrance) — position on wall, not just wall direction
- Anti-clockwise staircases (viewed from above)
- Dosha classification: Vata, Pitta, Kapha energy imbalances
- Remedies: mirror placement, crystal positioning, color therapy, deities
- Muhurat: auspicious timing for groundbreaking/key ceremonies
- Plot shape analysis: Square > Rectangle > L-shape (inauspicious) > T-shape (disaster)

**What they expect:**
- 16-zone Mandala overlay on canvas
- Brahmasthana detection & enforcement
- Entrance placement validation (position on E/N/NE wall, not just wall direction)
- Dosha severity levels + specific remedies
- Element (Pancha Bhuta) assignment per zone
- Staircase spiral direction validation (CCW from above)
- Height gradient enforcement (NE lower than SW)
- Water body/tank placement rules (NE ideal)
- Multi-floor Vastu (master bedroom must be ground floor)

**Why they'd reject it today:** Scoring is direction-only. Missing Brahmasthana, entrance, elements, doshas.

#### 🏗️ Civil / Structural Engineer
**Technical depth:** Expert  
**Domain skills they bring:**
- Load path analysis (gravity from roof → slabs → beams → columns → foundation)
- Foundation types: Raft, Pile, Strip, Isolated footing
- Seismic zone classification (India: Zone II-V)
- Material specification: M20/M25 concrete, Fe500 steel
- BOQ accuracy: steel tonnage, concrete volume, brick count, shuttering area
- Soil classification: SBC (Safe Bearing Capacity), expansive soils
- Wind load for glass area (IS 875 Part 3)
- Settlement risk zones

**What they expect:**
- Structural grid overlay with column placement tool
- Foundation type selector with bearing capacity calc
- Span limitation warnings (room > 30 ft without beam)
- Complete BOQ (steel, cement, aggregate, bricks) — not just construction cost
- Seismic input + recommendation
- Built-up vs carpet area differentiation
- Soil classification input

---

#### 🔧 MEP Engineer (Mechanical / Electrical / Plumbing)
**Technical depth:** Expert  
**Domain skills they bring:**
- Plumbing: gravity routing, wet area clustering, drainage slope (1:80 min)
- Water pressure: OHT (Overhead Tank) must be >10 ft above fixtures
- Electrical: load calculation per zone, panel sizing, ELCB/MCB requirements
- Fire safety: sprinkler zones, fire-rated walls (kitchen to living = 2hr rating)
- HVAC: split AC routing, fresh air CFM requirements
- Natural ventilation: cross-ventilation, minimum openable window area (1/10th floor area)
- Drainage: manholes, inspection chambers, septic tank positioning

**What they expect:**
- Wet area clustering validation (all bathrooms in one zone)
- Water flow/gravity routing visual
- Electrical load calculation per room
- Kitchen exhaust routing (must exit to outside, not attic)
- GFCI zone marking (within 6 ft of water sources)
- Fire-rated wall identification

---

#### 🪑 Interior Space Planner / Designer
**Technical depth:** Moderate  
**Domain skills they bring:**
- Furniture clearance standards: bed (36" circulation), sofa (24" between pieces), kitchen triangle
- Traffic flow: primary vs secondary circulation paths
- Room proportion: length should not exceed 2× width (Gestalt)
- Natural light: sun angles at 9AM/12PM/3PM/6PM by latitude
- Acoustic zoning: music room ≠ study/bedroom adjacency
- Storage density: minimum 10% of room area as storage
- Accessibility: 5 ft turning radius for wheelchair, grab bar zones
- Color psychology and material selection

**What they expect:**
- Furniture library (drag-in bed, sofa, kitchen triangle)
- Traffic flow validation (no dead-end corridors)
- Natural light simulation (sun angle × window placement)
- Clearance warnings (door swing conflicts, furniture reach space)
- Room proportion feedback ("This room is too narrow")

---

#### 📐 CAD / BIM Specialist
**Technical depth:** Expert technical  
**Domain skills they bring:**
- AIA Layer Naming Standard (e.g., `A-FLOR-ROOM`, `M-HVAC-SUPL`, `E-POWR-SWTC`)
- ISO 128 line weights (0.18mm detail, 0.5mm visible, 1.0mm cut)
- Drawing annotation: dimension strings, callouts, section marks, revision clouds
- Title block standards: project info, revision history, client/architect signature
- Block libraries: door swings (30°/45°/60°/90°), window types, fixture symbols
- Scale notation: 1:100 or 1/4" = 1'-0"
- Coordination: clash detection between architecture and MEP
- BIM Level of Development (LOD 100-500)
- File formats: DXF, DWG, IFC (BIM), RVT (Revit)

**What they expect:**
- AIA-compliant layer structure
- ISO line weights in DXF
- Door/window blocks with swing arcs
- Dimension strings as DXF DIMENSION entities
- Professional title block template
- Scale annotation
- Hatch patterns per material (concrete, brick, earth)
- DWG export (not just DXF)

**Why they'd reject DXF today:** Wrong layer names, no line weights, no door arcs, no dimension strings.

## 3. VASTU ENGINE — Authenticity Gaps

Current engine scores **~20% of real Vastu Shastra**. Critical gaps:

### 3.1 Missing Concepts (Ranked by Importance)

| Concept | Current | Required | Impact |
|---------|---------|----------|--------|
| **Brahmasthana** | Not implemented | Center ±10% must be void | Any room in center = invalid plan |
| **Entrance (Vastu Dwar)** | Not implemented | Position on wall, not just wall direction | Rule #1 in Vastu; completely ignored |
| **16-Zone Mandala** | 8 zones only | 16 zones (Shodasha Pada) | NE ≠ N ≠ NNE in Vastu |
| **Pancha Bhuta (5 Elements)** | Not implemented | SE=Fire, NE=Water, SW=Earth, NW=Air, Center=Space | Element conflicts = doshas |
| **Anti-clockwise staircase** | Direction only | Spiral direction validated | Clockwise = inauspicious per Vedic |
| **Height gradient** | Not implemented | NE corner lowest, SW highest | Slope rule for energy flow |
| **Water body/OHT placement** | Not implemented | NE or N only; SW = severe dosha | Critical for plumbing layout |
| **Multi-floor rules** | Each floor independent | Master bedroom = ground floor only | Vedic rule; 2nd-floor master = inauspicious |
| **Plot shape** | Not validated | Square > Rectangle > L > T (avoid) | Irregular plots need remedies |
| **Dosha severity** | Binary warning | Mild/Moderate/Severe/Critical | Practitioners need gradation |
| **Remedies** | None | Specific remedies per dosha | Differentiating feature |

### 3.2 Scoring Algorithm Issues

- **Angular distance wrapping** — East direction (0°) wraps to 360°; rooms at 350° are near-East but scored as near-West
- **No adjacency scoring** — Kitchen adjacent to master bedroom = dosha but not penalized
- **No zone overlap detection** — Room crossing a zone boundary gets only one zone scored
- **Score scale** — Linear penalty (−15 per 45°) is not how Vastu classifies zones (binary: ideal/acceptable/prohibited)

## 6. EXPORT QUALITY GAPS

### 6.1 PDF Issues
| Gap | Standard | Impact |
|-----|----------|--------|
| No scale annotation | Every professional drawing requires "1:100" or "1/4\" = 1'" | Can't use in construction |
| No north arrow in PDF (only canvas) | Standard on all site/floor plans | Orientation unknown |
| No legend (colors, Vastu zones) | Uninterpretable to third parties | Not client-presentable |
| A3 landscape (non-standard for floor plans) | A2 or A1 is standard for construction | Too small for detail |
| No revision block in title | Every drawing revision must be tracked | No version control on paper |
| Vastu warnings truncated at 60 chars | Full warning text critical for practitioners | Incomplete report |
| Cost rates not sourced | "Based on 2024 Bangalore estimates" missing | No credibility |
| No contingency/design fee | Standard +15% contingency + 10% design fee | Developer underestimates |

### 6.2 DXF Issues
| Gap | Standard | Impact |
|-----|----------|--------|
| Non-AIA layer names (`ROOM_LABELS` vs `A-FLOR-ROOM-NAME`) | AIA CAD Layer Guidelines 2019 | CAD workflow breaks |
| No line weights set | ISO 128: 0.18/0.35/0.5/0.7/1.0mm | All lines look same |
| No door/window symbols | Standard architectural symbols | Can't identify openings |
| Text labels only (no DIMENSION entities) | DXF DIMENSION for associative dims | Can't resize with model |
| Units not declared in DXF header | AutoCAD assumes inches if not set | 25× scale error |
| No hatch patterns | Brick/concrete/earth must be hatched | Unreadable sections |
| Rooms as 5-point polylines (not LWPOLYLINE) | Heavier file; less efficient | Larger files |
| Y-axis flip undocumented | Confusing for CAD users | Geometry appears mirrored |
